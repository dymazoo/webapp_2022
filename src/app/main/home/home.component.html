What is a Single Customer View (SCV) ?

You probably already have a lot of data about your customers, but it maybe scattered and difficult or impossible to use. Existing customers and their sales history could be in your On-line store, Ecommerce solution and sales history files. Contact information, like email address and phone number, may be in the Email Service Provider, often with duplication between email lists, or even mismatched and different data for the same person. There's also who you sent a specific marketing campaign to in the past, and their activity history, such as Opens, Clicks and Bounces. You may have data from on-line or physical events that you ran and who booked and/or attended. And many of our customers have a whole collection of files with all sorts of customer data, that they don't know what to do with.

The Dymazoo SCV Database takes all of this data and automatically matches and merges to create a unique 360 degree view of each customer. This includes Demographics (age, gender, location, interests, etc), Behavioral (opens, clicks, page views, etc) and Transactional (sales, event attendance, product returns, etc).

What is a Marketing Universe?

A marketing universe is the key to identifying your target audience for future marketing activities and for gaining insights into the behaviours of different groups of customers, that have similarities.

Each complete customer record in the SCV is analysed and augemented with information such as Recency, Frequency and Value related to that customer's Behavioural and Transactional activity. How many purchases have they made in the last 30, 60, 90 or more days? How often have they opened an email from a marketing campaign? What has the total value of the customer been, broken down by lifetime and by purchasing period. Further analysis makes use of the customer's Demographic data add information about the Quality of their record and to include them into categories, such "age is 18-25, 26-30, etc" or "gender is male, female or unknown".

A marketing universe is ever changing. As new data is added to the SCV and as time passes the derived information added to the SCV record also changes. So it's vital to rebuild the entire universe regularly.

How do Target Audiences and Segmentation help my business?

The augmented data in the marketing universe allows you to identify commonalities between customers and segment them into appropriate target groups. This might be something like "Woman under 40 that are interested in fashion and have a total sales value up to £200 in the past 90 days", "People that live within 100 miles of Birmingham, and have signed up to a webinar in the past year" or "Everyone that we have included in a specific marketing campaign, that opened but did not click". Each customer might belong to only a single target audience, or they may be in several different audiences.

Having identified target audiences, you can create Marketing Campaigns that offer products and services that are popular with some customers to other, similar customers, while at the same time not sending irrelevant messages to customers that the campaign would not interest.

You can also send different follow on campaigns to customers. For example by sending a compelling re-engagement offer to those customers that did not open, sending a call to action offer to those that did open but did not click and sending a "you might also like" offer to customers that went on to make a purchase from the initial campaign.

What is a typical Return on Investment (ROI) for targeted marketing?

Mass marketing (also known as "spray and pray") might reach a lot of people but most of them will take no action, or may even unsubscribe losing you any future opportunity to communicate with them. Targeted marketing is extremely cost-effective allowing you to connect with the right audience, with the right offer while only spending on campaigns that work.

ROI will vary from business to business, but studies show that on average, 77% of email marketing ROI comes from segmented, targeted campaigns.

"MailChimp reports that email opens and unique opens of segmented campaigns were 14.31% higher than non-segmented campaigns. Clicks from segmented campaigns were also more than 100% higher compared to non-segmented campaigns. Finally, when emails are segmented, there are 9.37% fewer un-subscribers" according to Marketing Insider Group.

What is a Single Customer View (SCV)?

You probably already have a lot of data about your customers, but it may be scattered and difficult, or even impossible, to make good use of. Existing Customer Data and their Sales History are in your On-line store, Ecommerce solution and Accounting system. Contact information, like email address and phone number, are in your Email Platform, often with Duplication between email lists, or worse, mismatched and different data for the same person. There's also who you sent a specific marketing campaign to in the past, and their activity history, such as Opens, Clicks and Bounces. You may have data from on-line or physical Events that you've hosted and who booked and/or attended. And many of our customers have a whole collection of Spreadsheets and Files with other customer data, that they simply aren't getting value from.

The Dymazoo CDP takes all of this data and automatically matches and merges to create a unique 360 degree view of each customer. This includes Demographics (age, gender, location, interests, etc), Behavioral (opens, clicks, page views, etc) and Transactional (sales, event attendance, product returns, etc).

What is a Marketing Universe?

A marketing universe is the key to identifying your target audiences for future marketing activities and for gaining insights into the behaviours and preferences of different groups of customers.

Each complete customer profile in the SCV Database is analysed and augmented with Added Value information, such as Recency, Frequency and Value related to that customer's Behavioural and Transactional activity. How many purchases have they made in the last 30, 60, 90 or more days? How often have they opened an email from a marketing campaign? What has the total value of the customer been, broken down by lifetime and by purchasing period. Further analysis makes use of the customer's Demographic data add information about the Quality of their record to allocate them to pre-built categories, such "age is 18-25, 26-30, etc" or "gender is male, female or unknown".

A marketing universe is ever changing. As new data is added to the SCV Database, and as time passes, the Added Value information in each customer profile also changes. This is why it's vital to rebuild the entire marketing universe regularly.

How can Target Audiences and Segmentation help my business?

The detailed and Added Value information in your marketing universe allows you to identify commonalities between customersand create segments, resulting in powerful, focused and appropriate target groups. This might be something like "Woman under 40 that are interested in fashion and have a total sales value up to £200 in the past 90 days", "People that live within 100 miles of Birmingham, and have signed up to a webinar in the past year" or "Everyone that we have included in a specific marketing campaign, that opened but did not click". Each customer might belong to only a single target audience, or they may be in several different audiences.

You can then combine, filter, or even exclude, people by target group to create highly focused audiences for your Marketing Campaigns that offer products and services that are relevant, popular and timely. While at the same time not sending irrelevant or unwelcome emails to customers that the campaign would not interest.

You can also send different Follow On campaigns to different customers. For example, sending a compelling re-engagement offer to those customers that did not open, a Call to Action offer to those that did open but did not click, and a "you might also like" offer to customers that went on to make a purchase from the initial campaign.

What is a typical Return on Investment (ROI) for targeted marketing?

Mass marketing (also known as "spray and pray") might reach a lot of people but most of them will take no action, or may even unsubscribe losing you any future opportunity to communicate with them. Targeted marketing is extremely cost-effective allowing you to connect with the right audience, with the right offer, at the right time, while only spending on campaigns that work.

ROI will vary from business to business, but studies show that on average, 77% of email marketing Return on Investment comes from segmented and targeted campaigns.

"MailChimp reports that email opens and unique opens of segmented campaigns were 14.31% higher than non-segmented campaigns. Clicks from segmented campaigns were also more than 100% higher compared to non-segmented campaigns. Finally, when emails are segmented, there are 9.37% fewer un-subscribers" according to Marketing Insider Group.

Effective email marketing yields a Return on Investment of up to $42 for every $1 spent
